\noindent\textbf{Data generation and sampling.}
We follow the Tic-Tac-Toe benchmark protocol of Meyer et al. Legal reachable board states are generated by expanding the game tree from the empty grid, alternating cross and circle moves, and stopping once a game is decided. This yields 5478 distinct boards, stored as cyclically indexed values $g_i\in\{-1,0,+1\}$ with the center last so the corner, side, and center fields match the $D_4$ orbits. Labels follow the winning-line rule, with unfinished positions counted as draws. For every seed and training size we draw class-balanced train and test subsets and reuse the same split for all models compared at that setting. Meyer et al.'s headline Tic-Tac-Toe setup uses 450 training examples and a fixed test set of 600 examples; our sweep keeps the same balanced-sampling rule and test size while varying the number of training examples.

\noindent\textbf{Reproducibility.}
Code, figure sources, and checked CSVs are available at \href{https://github.com/eybmits/qc_symmetry}{github.com/eybmits/qc\_symmetry}. All reported VQLM runs use one protocol: $L=3$, $p=2$, the paper single-qubit block, mean-squared error, Adam at learning rate $0.01$, batch size $15$, test size $600$, train sizes $30,60,120,240,450,600$, seeds $0$--$9$, and $100\times30$ optimizer updates. Figures regenerate with \texttt{python paper/make\_figures.py}; the manuscript rebuilds with \texttt{latexmk -pdf -interaction=nonstopmode main.tex}. Validation requires 360 edge rows, 120 edge+lines rows, 160 ablation rows, and 110 random-sharing rows; intervals are 95\% confidence intervals, and the rule oracle is a label sanity check, not a plotted baseline.
